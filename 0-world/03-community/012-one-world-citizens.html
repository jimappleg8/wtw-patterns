12 One-World Citizens

insert context statement

We are all citizens of planet Earth but we have divided Earth politically, physically and culturally.

To achieve the kinds of efficiencies that will be necessary for a World that Works, there must be a free exchange of ideas, people, and materials and information around the globe. Closed borders result in artificial scarcities.

Borders and citizenship came out of the concept that we had to divide the world into sovereign nations each defending their own portion of the Earth's resources. Borders define what resources are under each nation's control, and citizenship defines who has rights to those resources. Groups of people have fought over these resources for all of history and borders are designed to keep out the enemy. In some cases, this is still a real concern, but in many cases it is not and restrictions are unnecessarily harmful.

There are many reasons that we divide our Earth into different areas -- Independent Regions -- but taking those divisions too far can cause problems in itself. We need to find a balance where we think of ourselves as world citizens and we, in fact, enjoy many global freedoms of movement and information exchange, without abolishing borders altogether or trying to create some kind of politically-based global citizenship.

Indicators of global citizens:

  • opinion/statements from people about nationality
  • openness of borders
  • openness of information (even the internet is not 100% open)
  • personal connections across borders (personal and business relationships)

Therefore:

Encourage open borders as much as possible to give people the ability to move freely between political areas. Where this is impossible due to security issues, look to resolve the underlying causes of the conflict.
