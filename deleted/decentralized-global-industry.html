19 Decentralized Global Industry

with more of Earth's raw materials already in circulation -- Landfill Mines () -- our industries are going to have to be capable of adjusting to changing circumstances -- Industrialization (). This pattern explores one way that industry might be more agile.

In a world where large sources of raw materials are becoming more scarce, it will not always be possible to build a permanent factory near a source, and transporting materials over long distances is very inefficient.

Historically, factories were either built near a reliable source of raw materials or near transportation lines so they could receive materials from remote places.

This pattern refers to the idea that we can create small mini-factories that allow us to bring our industry closer to the sources of raw materials and keep production more local. It is a change from a monolithic centralized industrial model to a more distributed, decentralized model.

For example, Range Fuels, in Denver, Colorado, has designed a relatively small and mobile cellulose processing plant that produces ethanol and can be moved to wherever the source of cellulose is. This makes it possible for them to take advantage of relatively temporary sources, such as a tree die-off due to disease like we experienced here in the Rocky Mountains.

Therefore:

Consider the strategic advantage of decentralization in your business planning for emerging industries as well as established industries struggling to survive in a changing world.

As the workforce becomes more mobile -- Global Citizen () -- and global infrastructures are built to support industry in almost any location -- Global Energy Network (), Global Communications Network (), Global Transportation Network () -- this kind of approach will become more feasible
