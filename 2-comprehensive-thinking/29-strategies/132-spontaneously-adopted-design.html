132 Spontaneously Adopted Design

insert context statement

When we think we have the answer to a problem, it is tempting to try to convince -- or even force -- people to adopt our idea; doing so always leads to more suffering.

Rather than a strategy, this is really an indicator of successful design. If it is spontaneously adopted by people, then it is in line with evolution, acccording to Fuller.

Great ideas and designs will often take on a life of their own. People want to use them and will do so without being asked. That is at the heart of this pattern. Any time an idea requires external force to get people to adopt it, it is a strong indicator that something is wrong with the idea. To go ahead and push the idea is the epitome of arrogance, and can be very dangerous. In extreme cases, it leads to tyranny.

This is not to say that every good idea will be spontaneously adopted right away. If you are thinking ahead far enough -- Anticipated Change (140) -- the idea will likely be ahead of its time. You should be prepared for moving forward as soon as the need is recognized.

Therefore:

Have faith that if your idea is good enough, it will be adopted... eventually.
